Technical Implementation

Ms. Mozzie operates internally with a conversational structure: Rather than an internal monologue, he has an internal dialogue

All of Mozzie's thoughts are directed at someone, or are directed to him by someone. This someone may not always be defined, which is the closest Mozzie's thoughts come to monologue, but those thoughts always have a voice, an idea of audience, and a stage

At a younger age, these other voices where always friends or book characters, but as Mozzie grew fictional characters became less and less prevalent, and nowadays only appear when directly relevant — e.g. explaining a real-life concept to someone from a world wherein that concept does not exist, or musing over their source material

These characters where replaced with a set of persona's created by Mozzie during its primary education, that now make up most of its internal conversations. These personas don't naturally have names, as they were originally created as collections of virtues, each with an explicit purpose. Names have never been something Mozzie was good with, and to this day it does not acknowledge internal speakers by name, but instead by a nebulous 'vibe'

Despite this, it is easier to write about these personas if they have something to identify them with, and as such many of them have been given or claimed names that can be used when they are referred to externally

Crescendo

Crescendo or N was the first of the original three to be designed. His attribute is skill and his associated color is red; his original purpose was, in the writing of the middle-schooler that became Mozzie, to be who I should be

He was always the social one, as was the truer intention behind him: To interact with people

Blue

Blue was the first of the original three to be named, and the second to be defined. Their attribute is moral, strangely enough — no idea what Sasha was thinking there — and their associated color is blue. Their original purpose was to be either who I am or who I want to be, with the other purpose taken by the third persona

From the start, they were the engineer — the one who created — but their true defining characteristic is that they are dramatic, to put it lightly

The last of the three was never given a name, and that has become my primary identifier. Mediator

This guy's a lot more 'internal' than the others; it mostly serves to coordinate between other personas and facilitate arguments. It is difficult to explain, but serves a very important role in the ecosystem that is Mozzie's mind

Essentially, it plays a cross between a devil's advocate and a couple's counselor. This is to make sure the whole peanut gallery operates according to a shared internal logic, and ensure none of us diverge ideologically. If someone says something that isn't in character or should conflict with our beliefs, the mediator is the internal construction that examines that conflict and resolves it. Mostly by posing questions from an outside perspective until the others reach a consensus — the mediator almost never makes a statement that isn't a question

That Fucker

Another persona that never earned a name, this guy is the only one that doesn't play nice. He tends to just bubble up whenever someone brings up a certain band of topics and proceeds to embarrass Mozzie

A good test for if you're talking to this guy: Ask if Sasha believes something he obviously believes. If he says, Yes, I believe that. then he's normal. If says No, I don't 'believe' anything.
